Food Name Ingredients
Jollof Rice rice,tomato paste,fresh tomatoes,onions,peppers,chicken,vegetable oil,seasoning cubes

Jollof rice is a popular, vibrant West African one-pot dish made by cooking rice in a savory, spiced tomato and pepper sauce. Known for its rich, smoky flavor and deep red-orange color, it is commonly served at gatherings and is a point of culinary pride across countries like Nigeria, Ghana, and Senegal.

Serving : Typically served with fried plantains, salads, or proteins like grilled chicken or fish.

Step-by-Step Preparation

Prepare the Base: Blend the tomatoes, red bell peppers, onions, ginger, and garlic into a smooth puree.

Dry the Puree (Optional but Recommended): To reduce cooking time, boil the blended mixture in a pot until most of the water evaporates, making it a thick paste.

Prepare the Rice: Rinse the parboiled rice thoroughly in cold water until the water runs clear. This removes excess starch and prevents clumping.

Sauté Onions and Tomato Paste: Heat oil in a large pot. Add finely sliced onions and fry until translucent. Add the tomato paste and fry for 5–7 minutes until it turns dark, which removes the sour taste.

Fry the Pepper Mix: Add the blended pepper mixture to the fried paste and oil. Stir well and let it cook for 15-20 minutes until the oil starts separating from the sauce and it becomes a thick, rich stew.

Add Spices and Seasoning: Stir in the curry powder, thyme, bay leaves, bouillon cubes, and salt. Allow to cook for another 2–5 minutes to release the flavors.

Add Stock and Water: Pour in the chicken or beef stock and some water. Ensure the liquid is enough to cook the rice but not too much—the liquid should just barely cover the rice. Bring to a boil.

Add Rice and Cook: Add the rinsed rice to the pot and stir well so every grain is coated. Seal and Steam: Immediately reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid, or preferably, use aluminum foil to seal the pot first, then put the lid on to trap all steam.

Final Cooking: Cook on low heat for 25-30 minutes. Do not stir frequently.

Final Touches (Optional): When the rice is almost done, you can stir in butter, sliced onions, and fresh tomatoes for extra aroma and flavor. To achieve the "smoky" party flavor, you can increase the heat for the last 1–2 minutes to purposely burn the bottom slightly.

Rest and Serve: Turn off the heat and let the rice rest for 5–10 minutes, covered, before serving.
